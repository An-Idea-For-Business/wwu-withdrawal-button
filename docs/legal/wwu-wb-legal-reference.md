## 2. Directive 2011/83/EU — new Article 11a (verbatim)

Inserted by Directive (EU) 2023/2673. *"Exercise of the right of withdrawal by means of the withdrawal function in case of distance contracts concluded through an online interface."*

**[EN] Art. 11a(1)** — "For distance contracts concluded by the means of an online interface, the trader shall ensure that the consumer can also withdraw from the contract by using a withdrawal function. The withdrawal function shall be labelled with the words **'withdraw from contract here'** or an unambiguous corresponding formulation in an easily legible way. The withdrawal function shall be continuously available throughout the withdrawal period. It shall be prominently displayed on the online interface and easily accessible to the consumer."

**[EN] Art. 11a(2)** — "The withdrawal function shall enable the consumer to send an online withdrawal statement informing the trader of his or her decision to withdraw from the contract. That online withdrawal statement shall enable the consumer to easily provide or confirm the following information: (a) his or her name; (b) details identifying the contract from which he or she wishes to withdraw; (c) details of the electronic means by which the confirmation of the withdrawal will be sent to the consumer."

**[EN] Art. 11a(3)** — "Once the consumer has completed the online withdrawal statement in accordance with paragraph 2, the trader shall enable the consumer to submit it to him by means of a confirmation function. That confirmation function shall be labelled in an easily legible manner, and **only with the words 'confirm withdrawal'** or with an unambiguous corresponding formulation."

**[EN] Art. 11a(4)** — "Once the consumer activates the confirmation function, the trader shall send to the consumer an acknowledgement of receipt of the withdrawal on a durable medium, including its content and the date and time of its submission, without undue delay."

**[EN] Art. 11a(5)** — "The consumer shall be considered to have exercised his or her right of withdrawal within the relevant withdrawal period if he or she submitted the online withdrawal statement referred to in this Article before that period expired."

### Italian authentic version (EUR-Lex)
**[IT] Art. 11 bis(1)** — "Per i contratti a distanza conclusi mediante un'interfaccia online, il professionista provvede affinché il consumatore possa recedere dal contratto anche utilizzando una funzionalità di recesso. La funzionalità di recesso è indicata dalle parole **«recedere dal contratto qui»** o da una inequivocabile formulazione corrispondente in modo facilmente leggibile. La funzionalità di recesso è disponibile in maniera continuativa per tutto il periodo in cui può essere esercitato il diritto di recesso. Essa figura in modo ben visibile sull'interfaccia online ed è facilmente accessibile al consumatore."

**[IT] Art. 11 bis(3)** — "…il professionista consente al consumatore di presentarla mediante una funzionalità di conferma. Tale funzionalità di conferma deve essere indicata in modo facilmente leggibile, **e soltanto con le parole «conferma recesso»** o una formulazione altrettanto inequivocabile."

**[IT] Art. 11 bis(4)** — "Una volta attivata la funzionalità di conferma, il professionista invia al consumatore, senza indebito ritardo, un avviso di ricevimento del recesso su un supporto durevole, comprensivo del suo contenuto e della data e dell'ora della sua trasmissione."

### Recital 37 (controlling interpretation — verbatim EN)
"In addition to other existing means of withdrawal, such as the form laid down in Annex I(B) of Directive 2011/83/EU, where the trader offers the possibility of concluding distance contracts by means of an online interface, such as through a website or application, the trader should be obliged to enable the consumer to withdraw from such a contract via a function. This should ensure that consumers can withdraw from a contract just as easily as they can conclude it. […] the consumer should not have to undertake procedures to find or access the function, such as downloading an application if the contract was not concluded via that application. […] the trader could for example provide hyperlinks leading the consumer to the withdrawal function. […] a consumer who has already identified himself or herself, for example by logging in, should be able to withdraw from the contract without the necessity of providing once more their identification […]. In order to avoid the unintended exercise of the right of withdrawal […] the trader should require the consumer to confirm the decision to withdraw […]. If the consumer has ordered multiple goods or services within the same distance contract, the trader can provide the consumer with the possibility to withdraw from a part rather than the whole of the contract. […] the trader should communicate to the consumer an acknowledgement of receipt of such a withdrawal on a durable medium without undue delay. This obligation should apply not only to distance contracts for financial services, but to all distance contracts that are subject to the right of withdrawal under Directive 2011/83/EU. […]"

→ **Scope is general e-commerce**, not only financial services. The button is **additional** to the Annex I(B) model form.

## 3. Italy — Art. 54-bis Codice del Consumo (D.Lgs. 209/2025)

*"Esercizio del diritto di recesso dai contratti a distanza conclusi mediante un'interfaccia online."* (numbering: the directive's single Art. 11a is split into **7 commi**; the labelling/availability/prominence rule is **comma 3**.)

- **comma 1** — "Per i contratti a distanza conclusi mediante un'interfaccia online, il professionista consente al consumatore di recedere dal contratto anche utilizzando una funzione di recesso."
- **comma 2** — statement function with (a) nome; (b) informazioni che identificano il contratto; (c) mezzo elettronico per la conferma.
- **comma 3** ⚠️*(confirm punctuation vs Normattiva)* — function labelled «**recedere dal contratto qui**» (or equivalent unambiguous), legible, continuously available throughout the period, prominently displayed, easily accessible.
- **comma 4** — confirmation function after the statement.
- **comma 5** — confirmation labelled «**conferma recesso**» (or equivalent unambiguous).
- **comma 6** — acknowledgement on a **supporto durevole**, "comprensivo del suo contenuto e della data e dell'ora della sua trasmissione", *senza indebito ritardo*.
- **comma 7** — timely exercise if the online statement was submitted before the deadline.

Related Cod. Consumo articles: **art. 52** (14-day period; start = delivery for goods / conclusion for services-digital); **art. 53** (period extends to 12 months + 14 days if info missing); **art. 59** (exclusions); **art. 45 c.1 lett. l** (durable medium definition — email qualifies); **art. 27 c.9 / c.9-bis** (AGCM sanctions €5,000–€10,000,000 or 4% turnover, ex officio).

### Annex I(B) / Allegato I parte B — model withdrawal form (verbatim IT)
"**B. Modulo di recesso tipo** — (compilare e restituire il presente modulo solo se si desidera recedere dal contratto) — Destinatario [il nome, l'indirizzo geografico e l'indirizzo di posta elettronica devono essere inseriti dal professionista]: — Con la presente io/noi (*) notifichiamo il recesso dal mio/nostro (*) contratto di vendita dei seguenti beni/servizi (*) — Ordinato il (*)/ricevuto il (*) — Nome del/dei consumatore(i) — Indirizzo del/dei consumatore(i) — Firma del/dei consumatore(i) (solo se il presente modulo è notificato in versione cartacea) — Data — (*) Cancellare la dicitura inutile."

→ **Remains mandatory** in pre-contractual information; the button is cumulative, not a replacement.

## 4. Per-country statutory labels (the safe defaults)

The directive gives the labels in every official language; national transpositions may refine them. **Confirmation labels** must use *only* the statutory words (Art. 11a(3)).

| Country / locale | Withdrawal button | Confirmation button | National basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🇮🇹 IT | `recedere dal contratto qui` | `conferma recesso` | Art. 54-bis Cod. Consumo |
| 🇬🇧 EN (generic EU) | `withdraw from contract here` | `confirm withdrawal` | Dir. 2011/83 Art. 11a |
| 🇩🇪 DE | `Vertrag widerrufen` ⚠️ **no "hier"** | `Widerruf bestätigen` | **§356a BGB (n.F.)** |
| 🇫🇷 FR | `renoncer au contrat ici` | `confirmer la rétractation` | **Art. D.221-5** Code conso |
| 🇪🇸 ES | `desistir del contrato aquí` | `confirmar desistimiento` | Dir. (RDL 1/2007 ⚠️ pending) |

### Germany — the two-buttons trap
- **§356a BGB (n.F.)** = the NEW **Widerrufsbutton** (14-day withdrawal): "Die Widerrufsfunktion muss gut lesbar mit **„Vertrag widerrufen"** oder einer anderen gleichbedeutenden eindeutigen Formulierung beschriftet sein" (Abs. 1); confirmation "**„Widerruf bestätigen"**" (Abs. 3); durable-medium Eingangsbestätigung with content + date/time (Abs. 4).
- **§312k BGB** = the pre-existing **Kündigungsbutton** (terminate ongoing subscriptions): `Verträge hier kündigen` / `jetzt kündigen`. **Different feature — do not merge.** German withdrawal label has **no** "hier" (asymmetry vs IT/EN/FR/ES).

### France — labels live in the décret
- **L.221-21** Code conso (framework) requires a free, "facile, direct et permanent" rétractation functionality + durable-medium acknowledgement.
- **D.221-5** fixes the labels: `renoncer au contrat ici` + `confirmer la rétractation`; rejects ambiguous wording ("Annuler", "Demande de retour"). Missing any of the **6 cumulative requirements** (direct/permanent access, free, unambiguous labelling, two-step, no forced account creation, durable-medium receipt) → withdrawal window extends to **12 months + 14 days**. ("rétractation" = the 14-day distance right; "résiliation" = termination — keep distinct.)

### Spain — not transposed (use directive + direct effect)
RDL 1/2007 (LGDCU) arts. 102 ss. ("derecho de desistimiento") — Art. 11a **not yet transposed** as of 06/2026 (missed deadline). Ship directive Spanish labels (`desistir del contrato aquí` / `confirmar desistimiento`); consumers rely on **direct effect** from 19 June 2026. ⚠️ Re-verify on boe.es at release.

## 5. Cross-border applicability (Rome I)

The obligation follows the **consumer**, not the trader's establishment:
- **Dir. 2011/83 Recital 58** — "Where the law applicable to the contract is that of a third country, Regulation (EC) No 593/2008 [Rome I] should apply, to determine whether the consumer retains the protection granted by this Directive."
- **Rome I Art. 6(1)** — a consumer contract is governed by the law of the consumer's habitual residence where the professional "by any means, directs such activities to that country". **Art. 6(2)** — a choice of law cannot deprive the consumer of non-derogable protections.

**Consequences:**
- A trader **outside the EU** (Switzerland, UK, US) that **targets/sells to EU/EEA consumers MUST** provide the withdrawal button for those EU/EEA consumers. ✅ *confirmed vs EUR-Lex + Rome I.*
- A trader selling to a **Switzerland-resident** consumer is governed by Swiss law — **no statutory online withdrawal right** (CO/OR Art. 40a–40g = doorstep/telephone only; kmu.admin.ch: "Swiss law does not provide for any right to change your mind and return a product following an online purchase"). → CH-resident = **voluntary mode only**.
- **UK** (Consumer Contracts Regs 2013) — **no** withdrawal-button mandate; a UK trader selling to EU consumers must still provide it for those EU consumers.
- **EEA** (NO/IS/LI) — transpose via the EEA Agreement; ⚠️ confirm status; default in-scope.

## 6. GDPR posture for the immutable log
The log processes personal data (IP, contract data, timestamps). Basis: **Art. 6(1)(c)** (acknowledgement obligation) + **Art. 6(1)(f)** (audit/evidence). Requires an **Art. 30 record of processing** and a **DPIA** note. The plugin generates the privacy-policy clause + a records-of-processing snippet, and stores the **raw IP** (statutorily required as evidence — "data, ora, IP e dati contratto") with configurable retention (default 10 years).
